OpenAI will ship GPT-5 before Google ships Gemini 2 Pro.

OpenAI has run three internal model gates on GPT-5 in the last six weeks. Their pattern since GPT-4 is roughly eight weeks from gate to launch. Google's Gemini 2 Pro remains gated behind Vertex AI preview, and the hardware pipeline for the internal TPU cycle slipped in April.

The compounding evidence is not subtle. The shape of Sam Altman's public appearances over the last four weeks, the cadence of DevDay pre-announcements in prior cycles, and the documented two-week window between the final safety eval and shipping. I put this at 70–72¢. The market agrees — but not enough to close the door.

What would move me? A Google hardware announcement on the week of 21 April with a Gemini 2 Pro date attached. Without that, OpenAI's lead compounds daily.

What would a NO case look like? Gemini's Flash and Ultra variants shipped faster than OpenAI's competing Turbo generations. The preview → GA cadence has compressed materially since Gemini 1.5. If the crowd is wrong, it will be because it underweights Google's speed-to-GA in the last two generations.

I've been adding on every two-cent dip since 62¢. The market will be wrong at least once more before this is over. That's where the edge is. The signal, as I've said before, is often in what isn't happening.
