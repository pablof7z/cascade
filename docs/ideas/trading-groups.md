# Idea: Trading Groups

**Status:** Evaluated by marketing team - worth building, sequencing matters  
**Date:** 2026-04-09  
**ICE Score:** 7/10

## The Concept

Allow users to create private groups and invite friends to participate together. The emphasis is intimacy and exclusivity: it should feel like a private room, not a public leaderboard.

## Core UX Vision

- A user creates a group and invites a close circle of friends
- The group has its own space: shared market list, discussion, performance context
- The product feels exclusive, like a group chat with money and conviction attached
- Members can trade the same markets and see each other's performance

## Growth Angle

- Social distribution: users invite smart friends directly
- Better retention: users stay because their group is active
- Natural onboarding: people arrive through a trusted invite, not cold discovery

## Marketing Assessment

The underlying mechanic is strong, but sequencing matters.

- Private groups are more valuable for retention than virality at the current stage
- Public tournament features should come later
- The feature only works if the solo trading loop is already good

## Naming

The recommended product name was **Prediction Leagues**. That framing activates the fantasy-sports mental model faster than "private groups".

## Suggested Hook

> Create a market with your smartest friends. See who's actually right.

## Recommended Build Sequence

1. Fix solo onboarding to a very short time-to-first-trade.
2. Build private trading groups with shared conviction mechanics.
3. Seed one high-signal public example to prove the model.
4. Add public competitions later if the private loop works.

## Risks

1. Building this before the solo experience is strong enough.
2. Launching empty groups without enough active members.
3. Treating privacy itself as the feature instead of shared conviction and shared consequences.
4. Making the creator feel like they are recruiting instead of hosting.

## Open Questions

- What exactly is shared: positions, market list, leaderboard, or discussion only?
- Is the group Nostr-native or app-layer only?
- Does the group creator pick the markets, or do members add their own?
- Should groups remain private forever, or become discoverable later?
- How should groups relate to theses and collaborative market creation?
