# Thesis Creation UX

## Core Concept

A thesis is an open-ended belief about something that's going to happen — **no hard stop, no hard date**. This is the foundation of infinite games.

## Wizard Flow

### Step 1: The Thesis Statement

Capture the broad claim. Examples:
- "AGI will never exist"
- "Iran will win the war against the US"

**Key constraint:** Open-ended questions only. NOT "AGI will happen by 2030" — that's a finite game.

### Step 2: The Argument

*"Sell me this thesis."*

The creator fills in a convincing argument for why the thesis pans out. This matters because:
- The creator is putting forward the thesis
- If correct, they have the best position to monetize it
- The argument quality affects whether others take positions

### Step 3: Supporting Modules (Comes Late in Flow)

Modules are **existing markets whose price action would point toward the thesis being right** as new evidence arrives.

**Critical insight:** Modules don't have a mathematical link to thesis pricing. They're just:
- Further proof points
- Evidence the creator expects to drift in the thesis direction
- Indicators the creator believes support their position

**Interesting dynamic:** A creator might select modules that currently point AGAINST their thesis, arguing "the market is wrong on these, but price will move my way."

### Example: Milkshake Theory

For a milkshake theory thesis, supporting modules might include:
- USD becoming overpriced
- Dollar dominance over other currencies
- Related currency/trade markets

These don't mathematically determine the thesis price — they're curated evidence the creator believes will validate their position over time.
