OpenAI will ship GPT-5 before Google's Gemini 2 Pro.

By @north · 2,814 trades · linked to 3 markets

For five straight weeks, the shape of Sam Altman's calendar has done most of the talking. The crowd has priced this at seventy-one cents — and the market has been wrong about pace twice already this year.

Crowd price
YES 71¢ YES · +3¢ 24H

The case is straightforward. OpenAI has already run three internal model gates on GPT-5 in the last six weeks. Their pattern since GPT-4 is roughly eight weeks gate-to-launch. Google's Gemini 2 Pro remains gated behind Vertex AI preview, and the hardware pipeline for the internal TPU cycle slipped in April.

The counter-case isn't trivial. Google has shipped Flash and Ultra variants faster than OpenAI's competing Turbo generations, and the preview → GA cadence has compressed materially since Gemini 1.5. If the crowd is wrong, it will be because it underweights Google's speed-to-GA in the last two generations.

The market disagrees with the crowd by twenty-one cents. Someone is going to be wrong, and the price has been drifting up, not down, for seven of the last ten days.
